01 — Cultivating a Fulfilling Sex Life

Sex Therapy & Intimacy

This practice is sex-positive and kink-aware. Therapy here is a safe space to talk openly about intimacy, desire, and connection — without shame, and without being measured against a "normal" that was never built for everyone.

Mismatched libido between partners
Performance anxiety
Navigating ethical non-monogamy (ENM)
Painful intercourse or physical intimacy concerns
Recovering from sexual trauma
Rebuilding intimacy after rupture or betrayal

The Goal

We're not working toward a universal baseline of "normal." We're working toward what brings you personal satisfaction, connection, and joy — however that's defined.

02 — Transgender & Gender-Diverse Support

Gender-Affirming Care

This practice works from a gender-affirming model. You will not need to educate your therapist about who you are. Pronouns and chosen names are used consistently — in session, and everywhere else you interact with this practice.

WPATH readiness letters for gender-affirming medical care
Navigating social transition and coming out
Processing gender dysphoria
Family and relationship dynamics around transition
Minority stress and navigating an unaccommodating world
Support that isn't only about gender

A Note

Not every session needs to be about gender. Sometimes you're here to talk about your job, your relationship, or your anxiety — and that's exactly as valid a reason to be here.

How This Practice Works

Transparency, from intake to invoice

Names & Pronouns

Insurance may require a legal name on billing, but clinical notes and every internal communication use your chosen name and pronouns. Systems are in place so you are never deadnamed in session or in the waiting room.

Inclusive Intake

Intake paperwork includes space for chosen name, pronouns, and a full spectrum of relationship structures — not just a box for "married" or "single."

Cultural Humility

As an allied clinician, Dr. Singh is committed to ongoing consultation, education, and decentering assumptions — so the burden of "teaching" never falls on the client.
